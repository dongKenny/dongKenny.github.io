Japanese Dialects

This is a project which aims to (non-exhaustively) showcase the differences between "Standard Japanese" (標準語 - hyōjungo), illustrate grammatical changes, and highlight interesting colloquialisms

Hyōjungo

The "textbook" Japanese which became standardized after the Meiji Restoration.

Meiji Restoration in 1868, the government attempted to create a strong centralized state. Linguistic unification was a step toward shaping the national identity. Through the national educational system and the military, a dominant national dialect replaced local and regional dialects. The resulting dialect, hyōjungo (‘the standard language’), was based on the linguistic patterns of Tōkyō’s samurai (‘warrior’) classes and has become the norm in the educational system, the mass media, government, and business.

Theodore Bestor, Japan in Countries and their Cultures Vol. 2

Gramamtical Changes

Grammar Change Standard Form Dialect Form
だ --> や 猫だね 猫やね
じゃ --> や 猫じゃない！ 猫やない！
ない --> ん 知らない 知らん
い --> か (adj) 面白い 面白か
よ --> ばい それだよ それやばい
